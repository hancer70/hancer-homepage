About

I am a Professor at the Rosen College of Hospitality Management, University of Central Florida, with over two decades of experience specializing in the intersection of human resources, technology, and social media within hospitality.

My work is defined by a commitment to advancing knowledge and fostering innovation in hospitality management. My research encompasses a broad spectrum of topics, including employee motivation, digital transformation in hospitality, medical tourism, and sustainable consumer behavior. My recent studies, published in top-tier journals such as the International Journal of Hospitality Management and Journal of Hospitality and Tourism Research, offer critical insights into workforce dynamics, digital marketing strategies, and eco-conscious tourism practices.

Throughout my academic career, I have focused on research that combines theoretical rigor with practical relevance, aiming to address pressing challenges faced by the hospitality industry. This dedication has been recognized through numerous awards, including the Outstanding Graduate Mentor award at Oklahoma State University and nominations for Faculty Excellence in Mentoring at UCF. I have consistently engaged in funded research, leading projects that explore employee retention, the integration of AI and social media within hospitality settings, and cybersecurity in senior living communities.

In addition to my research, I am deeply committed to education and mentorship, guiding students in their academic pursuits and professional development. My teaching philosophy centers on creating inclusive and dynamic learning environments that encourage critical thinking, experiential learning, and interdisciplinary collaboration. I take pride in supporting the next generation of hospitality leaders, not only through classroom instruction but also by fostering research collaborations that contribute to our field's body of knowledge.

As a researcher, mentor, and educator, my goal is to bridge academic insights with industry applications, enhancing both the theoretical and practical dimensions of hospitality management.
